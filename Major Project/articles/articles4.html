Do I need protein shakes?

With interest in protein shakes and powders growing more every year, questions around usage, benefits, training supplementation, and how much is too much are more common than ever. Keep reading to learn more about protein powders and muscle growth, and whether they’re a viable option to help you reach your training goals.

What is a protein shake?

You can buy readymade protein shakes or purchase a bag of powder to make your own at home. Protein powder can be dissolved and mixed with milk, water, juice or certain types of food to enhance your daily protein intake. The benefits of protein powder drinks are that they are quick, easy, and are a great source of protein for people who exercise regularly and find it hard to get 100% of their protein from food alone. Most people take a protein shake after the gym to avoid eating a full meal before bed. They’re also a common supplementation for those of us with specific dietary requirements who struggle to meet their daily protein intake from food alone.

Understanding the value of pure protein

There are many different types of protein powder, so it's important you get the right one. Pure proteins are exactly what they say they are. They are proteins that are free of additives. Pure protein powders include:

1. Whey protein powders

2. Pea protein powders

3. Hemp protein powders

4. Soya powders

5. Egg powders

These powders can be mixed with water, one of your five fruit or veg, or juice. You can even add your protein powder to porridge. Doing this is a useful way for someone intolerant to the common breakfast proteins of eggs and yoghurt to supplement their protein intake at the start of the day.

How much powder is right for me?

How much powder to take and whether you should drink protein shakes everyday is a commonly asked question. Using the information above, you should now know how much protein you need to meet your goals. Whilst there’s nothing wrong with having a shake or protein smoothie every day, you should aim to get as much of your protein from your diet as possible. You can then supplement where necessary with shakes, bars, and powders. Bottled protein shakes typically contain between 20g and 30g of protein. Powders that you take yourself differ because you can use as much or as little as you like, however a typical protein powder scoop will provide roughly the same amount of protein per serving as a readymade shake.

Do I need protein powder if I work out?

Absolutely not. Because of the close marketing link between the gym and protein supplements, powders and supplements are often viewed as a necessary tool for working out. This couldn’t be further from the case. Protein powder is a great way for people who train hard to supplement their hard work in the gym. There are many other factors that influence muscle growth which are far more important than your protein supplementation. If you’re looking to build muscle, adopt a regimented weightlifting routine, ensure you’re eating a healthy diet rich in macronutrients, and prioritise rest and recovery in between workouts.

How much protein do I need in a day?

The amount of protein you need depends on a variety of factors like age, sex, activity level, and your goals. As a general guideline, active individuals looking to maintain or build muscle should aim for between 0.7 and 1 gram of protein per pound of body weight (or 1.6 to 2.2 grams per kilogram of body weight). If weight loss is your focus, this number is slightly different. Those focusing on weight loss should aim for the higher end of this range to help preserve muscle mass whilst losing fat. These numbers are a rough guide and are no replacement for a tailored made plan by a dietician or nutritionist. Before undertaking any change to your diet, always consult a healthcare professional to assess what changes are best for you and your body.
